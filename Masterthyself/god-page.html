His Many Names

Every Name Reveals Something

In the ancient world, a name was not just a label — it was an identity, a nature, a promise. God's names throughout Scripture are not titles. They are self-revelations.

יהוה
Yahweh / Jehovah
The Self-Existent One. "I AM WHO I AM." The name so sacred ancient Israelites wouldn't speak it aloud. It means He exists by His own nature — He needs nothing, depends on nothing, owes His existence to no one.
Exodus 3:14 — "I AM has sent me to you."
אֱלֹהִים
Elohim
The All-Powerful Creator. The very first name used for God in Scripture — "In the beginning, Elohim created." It carries the idea of supreme authority and boundless creative power. The plural form suggests the fullness of His being.
Genesis 1:1 — "In the beginning, God created..."
אֲדֹנָי
Adonai
Lord and Master — but a master who is also protector. Used when people wanted to honor His authority over every area of their life. Recognizing God as Adonai is recognizing that He is not a force you control, but a Person you trust.
Psalm 8:1 — "O Adonai, our Lord, how majestic is Your name..."
אֵל שַׁדַּי
El Shaddai
God Almighty — specifically, the God who is more than enough. The name given to Abraham when God made a covenant with him. It speaks of God's ability to do the impossible, to bring life where there is barrenness, to fulfill every promise regardless of circumstances.
Genesis 17:1 — "I am El Shaddai — walk before Me and be blameless."
אַבָּא
Abba / Father
The most intimate of all His names — the Aramaic word a child uses for their father. Tender, personal, close. Jesus taught his disciples to begin prayer with this word. It signals that the God of the universe wants not worship from a distance, but relationship up close.
Romans 8:15 — "You received the Spirit of adoption by whom we cry out, 'Abba, Father.'"
עִמָּנוּאֵל
Immanuel
God With Us. Perhaps the most astonishing name in all of Scripture. The infinite God chose to be near — not observing from a distance, not managing from above, but present. With you. This name was given to Jesus at His birth and it remains the defining promise of His nature.
Matthew 1:23 — "They shall call His name Immanuel, which means God with us."
יהוה רֹעִי
Yahweh Rohi
The LORD is My Shepherd. A name of profound tenderness — the image of a shepherd who knows each sheep by name, who seeks the lost one, who lays down his life for the flock. This is not distant governance. This is intimate care for you specifically.
Psalm 23:1 — "The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want."
יהוה רָפָא
Yahweh Rapha
The LORD Who Heals. Not a God who observes your brokenness from a safe distance — but One who heals. Body, mind, emotions, past wounds, present pain. This name was first revealed after bitter waters were made sweet. He specializes in restoration.
Exodus 15:26 — "I am the LORD who heals you."
יהוה יִרְאֶה
Yahweh Jireh
The LORD Will Provide. Named by Abraham at the moment God provided a ram instead of his son Isaac. It became a declaration of trust for every impossible situation — that before the need arises, God has already prepared the answer. He sees what you cannot see yet.
Genesis 22:14 — "On the mountain of the LORD it will be provided."
אַלְפָּא וְאֹומֶגָא
Alpha & Omega
The First and the Last — the beginning of the story and its ending. Nothing precedes Him. Nothing continues after Him without His hand. This name, from the book of Revelation, declares that all of history — your life included — is held within His beginning and His end.
Revelation 22:13 — "I am the Alpha and the Omega, the First and the Last."
הֶדֶּרֶךְ
The Way, Truth & Life
Jesus' most complete self-description. Not "I show you the way" but "I AM the Way." Not "I teach truth" but "I AM the Truth." Not "I can give you life" but "I AM the Life." Three of the deepest human hungers — direction, certainty, aliveness — answered in one Person.
John 14:6 — "I am the way, the truth, and the life."
שַׂר שָׁלֹום
Prince of Peace
The Hebrew word shalom doesn't just mean the absence of conflict — it means wholeness, completeness, everything as it should be. Jesus brings a peace the world cannot give and cannot take away — a settled stillness in the soul even in the middle of chaos.
Isaiah 9:6 — "His name will be called... Prince of Peace."

His Nature

What Kind of God Is He?

Before you can trust someone, you need to know their character. Here is who God is — not as theology, but as lived reality.

♾️
Eternal
He exists outside of time. Before the universe was created, He was. After it ends, He will be. This means your life — which feels bounded by birth and death — is fully known by Someone who sees its beginning and end simultaneously. Nothing catches Him off guard.
Psalm 90:2 — "Before the mountains were born... from everlasting to everlasting, You are God."
👁️
Omniscient
He knows everything — not as a surveillance system, but as a lover who pays attention. He knows your thoughts before you think them, your pain before you name it, your needs before you ask. This is not invasion. This is the most complete being-known that exists.
Psalm 139:4 — "Before a word is on my tongue, You know it completely."
🌍
Omnipresent
There is nowhere you can go where He is not. Not the darkest moment of your life, not the most isolated place on earth, not the depths of your shame. He is already there. This was David's discovery in Psalm 139 — not frightening, but ultimately deeply comforting.
Psalm 139:8 — "If I make my bed in the depths, You are there."
⚡
Omnipotent
Nothing is too hard for Him. Not your situation, not your past, not your broken relationships, not the impossible dream in your heart. He created the universe from nothing. He raised the dead. Whatever you're facing — He is not overwhelmed by it.
Jeremiah 32:27 — "I am the LORD, the God of all mankind. Is anything too hard for me?"
❤️
Love
Not a God who loves — but a God who IS love (1 John 4:8). This is His nature, not just His behavior. His love is unconditional — not based on your performance, your goodness, or your worthiness. The cross is the proof: He loved you when you were at your worst, not your best.
Romans 5:8 — "While we were still sinners, Christ died for us."
⚖️
Just
Every wrong will be made right. Every injustice addressed. Every tear accounted for. God's justice is not cold — it is the burning passion of a Father who will not let evil have the final word. And remarkably, His justice and His mercy met at the cross rather than canceling each other out.
Deuteronomy 32:4 — "He is the Rock, His works are perfect... a God of faithfulness and without injustice."
🕊️
Holy
Holiness means He is utterly set apart — completely without darkness, without shadow, without inconsistency. What He calls good is genuinely good. What He calls true is genuinely true. His character is the foundation of reality itself. This is why His presence transforms everything it touches.
Isaiah 6:3 — "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD Almighty; the whole earth is full of His glory."
🌅
Faithful
He has never broken a promise. Not one. Every covenant He made in Scripture — with Noah, Abraham, David, the world — He kept. He cannot lie. He cannot change. The person you encounter today is the same Person who parted the Red Sea, who raised Lazarus, who rose on the third day. He does not diminish.
Lamentations 3:23 — "His mercies are new every morning; great is Your faithfulness."
🌱
Good
Not just occasionally good — fundamentally, unconditionally, relentlessly good. Even in the seasons when life is hard, when answers don't come, when prayer seems to bounce off the ceiling — His goodness is not in question. It is His nature. Taste and see, the Psalmist says. This requires trying.
Psalm 34:8 — "Taste and see that the LORD is good; blessed is the one who takes refuge in Him."

Right Now · Today · This Moment

What God Is Doing

God is not idle. He is not watching from a distance. Here is what Scripture reveals about His activity right now — in the world and in you.

🙏
He is interceding for you
Right now, Jesus — seated at the right hand of the Father — is actively interceding on your behalf. This is not metaphor. This means Someone with all authority in heaven and earth is speaking your name to the Father. Not accusing you. Advocating for you.
Hebrews 7:25 — "He always lives to intercede for them."
🔨
He is completing a work in you
God is not finished with you. He began something when you were born — and He is committed to completing it. Your struggles are not signs of abandonment. They are often the very tools He uses. The Potter doesn't throw away the clay when it resists — He keeps working it.
Philippians 1:6 — "He who began a good work in you will carry it on to completion."
🌐
He is holding all things together
Colossians 1:17 says "in Him all things hold together." This is not poetry — it means the physical coherence of the universe, the faithfulness of natural law, the continued existence of every atom, is sustained by His active will. The moment He stopped, everything would dissolve. He does not stop.
Colossians 1:17 — "In Him all things hold together."
🔍
He is searching for the lost
Jesus told three parables in a row in Luke 15 — the lost sheep, the lost coin, the lost son. The point of all three is the same: God does not wait for the lost to find their way home. He goes searching. The shepherd leaves the ninety-nine. The father runs down the road. He is the initiator, not the waiter.
Luke 15:4 — "Does he not leave the ninety-nine... and go after the lost sheep?"
🔄
He is making all things new
Revelation 21:5 is declared before the final age — "I am making everything new." Not "I made everything new" (past tense) or "I will make everything new" (distant future) — but present tense. The renewal is in process. Every act of healing, reconciliation, and restoration in this world is a foretaste of the complete renewal He is working toward.
Revelation 21:5 — "I am making everything new!"
💌
He is drawing people to Himself
Jesus said "No one can come to me unless the Father draws them" (John 6:44). The very desire to know God, the curiosity that brought you to this page, the hunger that makes you ask questions — that is not just your seeking. That is Him drawing you toward Himself. The longing and the source of the longing are the same.
John 6:44 — "No one can come to me unless the Father who sent me draws them."

Written and Kept

His Promises to You

The Bible contains over 3,000 promises. These are not wishes or suggestions. They are covenant commitments from Someone who cannot lie and has never failed to keep His word.

"
For I know the plans I have for you — plans to prosper you and not to harm you, plans to give you a hope and a future.
Jeremiah 29:11
God has a specific, good, intentional plan for your life. Not a vague wish — an actual plan. Written before you were born. This was spoken to people in exile — in the worst circumstances of their lives — which means it's true precisely when it's hardest to believe.
"
Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest.
Matthew 11:28
This is a direct invitation — not a suggestion for the religious. "All" means all. The condition is not goodness or worthiness. The condition is weariness. If you are tired, you qualify. The rest He gives is not just physical — it is the rest of a soul that has found where it belongs.
"
And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love Him.
Romans 8:28
"All things" means all things — including the painful ones, the confusing ones, the ones that seem senseless. This is not a promise that everything will feel good. It is a promise that nothing is wasted. He is a God who redeems — who takes broken things and makes them into something better than before.
"
I will never leave you nor forsake you.
Hebrews 13:5
The Greek original uses five negatives to make this as emphatic as possible — literally "I will never, no never, no never forsake you." The most absolute statement of permanence in Scripture. Every person who has ever abandoned you, let you down, or left — He is the one who stays.
"
Ask and it will be given to you; seek and you will find; knock and the door will be opened to you.
Matthew 7:7
Three verbs, all present tense, all active — keep asking, keep seeking, keep knocking. This is not a slot machine. It is an invitation into persistent, relational conversation with a Father who responds. The promise is not that you'll always get what you ask for. It is that the door always opens.
"
He will wipe every tear from their eyes. There will be no more death or mourning or crying or pain.
Revelation 21:4
This is the ultimate promise — the end of all suffering, personally. Not swept away from above, but wiped away personally, individually. Every tear you have ever cried is known. Not one is forgotten. And one day — not metaphorically but actually — they will be gone.

For Your Everyday Life

God in the Ordinary

God is not only found in churches, crises, or mountaintop moments. Here is where He actually shows up — in the texture of ordinary days.

🌅
In the Morning
His mercies are new every morning — not accumulated from yesterday, not borrowed from tomorrow. Each day begins fresh. Whatever yesterday held, today is a clean page.
😔
In Grief
Jesus wept at Lazarus's tomb — even knowing He would raise him. God doesn't skip over your grief. He enters it with you. "Blessed are those who mourn, for they will be comforted."
😰
In Anxiety
"Cast all your anxiety on Him because He cares for you" — the word "cast" means to throw violently. He doesn't want polite prayers. He wants you to hurl your fears at Him and let Him carry them.
🤔
In Confusion
"If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask of God, who gives generously." No lecture, no judgment — just generosity. He is not annoyed by your not-knowing. He is the answer to it.
💪
In Weakness
"My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness." The counterintuitive promise — His strength shows up most clearly when yours runs out. Not despite your weakness. Through it.
🎉
In Joy
He invented joy. "In Your presence is fullness of joy." Celebrations, laughter, delight, beauty — these are not distractions from God. They are glimpses of His nature, echoes of what He is and what He's building toward.
😔
In Failure
Peter denied Jesus three times — and Jesus personally restored him with three questions. He doesn't abandon you when you fall. He comes looking for you in the exact place you failed and offers the same conversation: "Do you love me?"
⏳
In Waiting
"Those who wait upon the LORD shall renew their strength." The waiting is not wasted time — it is the process. The formation. The deepening. God is not slow; He is thorough. He is preparing something you could not receive if it came any sooner.

For I am convinced that neither death nor life, neither angels nor demons, neither the present nor the future, nor any powers, neither height nor depth, nor anything else in all creation, will be able to separate us from the love of God that is in Christ Jesus our Lord.

Romans 8:38–39
